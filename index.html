School Projects

Quantum Leap

During sophomore year, I did one of the projects named 'Quantum Leap'. In Quantum Leap, we were required to, over the course of 9 weeks, create a video game with a group of 3 other people. The game was supposed to illustrate 4 different time periods, one for each group member.

A video game is a work-intensive project, requiring dozens of hours of programming and hundreds of art assets, in our case pixel art sprites. I did the programming while the other 3 people in my group did the art. After 9 weeks of work, we had a demo featuring most of the mechanics and characters we had planned.

We named the game Planet Busters:

These are the four playable characters we had planned, one for each time period. Although the viking character (third from the left) was not implemented due to technical and time constraints, every other character was implemented completely. Our game was based on combat, each character had their own unique combat abilities and weapons. As programmer, I was responsible for implementing these effects practically, while the artists drew the characters and weapons.

Going Viral

During junior year, I did another project named 'Going Viral'. In this project, we didn't have many constraints, just that we had to solve a problem relating to natural disasters. Our teacher's definiton of 'natural disaster' was very broad, so my group decided to focus on the skin cancer epidemic.

If you don't know, UV radiation from the sun can cause skin cancer. Most of the time, when people do especially sun-exposed activities, such as going to the beach, we wear sunscreen. However, most people don't know that you can reduce the risk of skin cancer by wearing sunscreen in daily use when most wouldn't think to.

We decided that a good way to solve this problem within the constraints of a school project was to target young people (ages 10-20) by creating a video game. Maybe you've noticed that video games are a popular medium for high school students to use for school projects. Anyways, this video game would teach young people about the dangers of skin cancer and how to prevent it with sunscreen and other methods.

This time around, I decided to let another person in my group handle the programming and I focused on research, as well as creating the website for the game, using HTML and CSS, which you can find here: https://skincancerpreventionprogram.github.io
